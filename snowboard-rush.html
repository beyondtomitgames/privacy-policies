Privacy Policy

App: Snowboard Rush
Last updated: April 4, 2026

1. Who we are

Snowboard Rush is developed and published by Tomit. If you have any questions about this policy, contact us at: support@tomit.app

2. What data we collect

We collect the minimum data necessary to operate the app:

  • Game preferences and progress — settings such as audio volume, joystick side, and completed level progress. Stored locally on your device only and never transmitted to our servers.

We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, location, or any other personally identifiable information directly.

3. Advertising (Google AdMob)

This app displays ads provided by Google AdMob. AdMob may collect and use data to show relevant ads, including:

  • Advertising ID (resettable device identifier)
  • IP address
  • Device and app usage information

Before ads are loaded, we request your consent in accordance with applicable privacy laws (including GDPR). You can change your consent choice at any time in the app settings.

For more information on how Google uses data, see Google's Privacy Policy.

4. Data storage and security

All game data (preferences, progress) is stored only on your device using local storage. We do not upload it to any server. If you uninstall the app, this data is deleted.

5. Data sharing

We do not sell, trade, or share your personal data with third parties, except as described above (AdMob, Google Play) for the purpose of operating the app.

6. Children

This app is intended for users aged 13 and older. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

7. Your rights

Since all game data is stored locally on your device, you have full control over it. You can delete it by uninstalling the app. For rights related to AdMob data, please refer to Google's privacy controls.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. We will notify you of significant changes by updating the "Last updated" date above. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the new policy.
